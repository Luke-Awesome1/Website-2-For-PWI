The Pygymy Tribe

Background Info

THE PYGMY TRIBE is an ethnic group, native to areas of Africa and Asia, whose average height is unusually short. There are atleast a dozen Pygmy groups, sometimes unrelated to each other. The best known are-

  • The Mbenga(Aka and Baka) of the Western Congo Basin
  • The Mbuti(Efe etc.) of the Ituri Rainforest
  • The Twa of the African Great Lakes

Most pygmy communities are partially hunter-gatherers.

This means that while these people do hunt for food and scavenge for fruits and vegetables, they are only partially, not exclusively, dependent on the same. They trade with neighbouring farmers to acquire cultivated foods and other material items; no group lives deep in the forest without access to agricultural products. It is estimated that there are between 250,000 and 600,000 Pygmies living in the Congo rainforest. However, although Pygmies are thought of as forest people, the groups called Twa may live in open swamp or desert.

The term pygmy, as used to refer to diminutive people, derives from Greek pygmaios via Latin Pygmaei (sing. Pygmaeus), meaning a short forearm cubit, or a measure of length corresponding to the distance from the wrist to the elbow.
